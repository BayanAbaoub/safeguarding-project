How to keep your family safe in the digital age

What is safeguarding?

Safeguarding is the process of protecting children, young people, and vulnerable adults from abuse, harm, and neglect. It involves creating a safe environment where individuals can thrive and reach their full potential. Safeguarding encompasses a range of practices, policies, and procedures designed to prevent harm and respond effectively to concerns.

Warning Signs

Recognizing the signs of abuse and neglect is crucial in safeguarding individuals. Here are some common indicators:

If there is a change in device use with increased secretive behaviour, such as locking the device or moving it when others approach, it may be a sign of potential issues.

If an individual exhibits sudden mood swings, such as distress, anxiety, or anger after using online devices, it may indicate potential problems.

If an individual becomes increasingly withdrawn, avoids social interactions, or shows a lack of interest in previously enjoyed activities, it may be a sign of digital abuse.

Take Action

If you suspect that someone is being abused or neglected, it is important to take action. Here are some steps you can take:

Report

It's important to report any concerns you may have about abuse or neglect to the relevant authorities, such as social services or the police.

Document

Document any evidence or observations that support your concerns.

Support

Offer support to the individual if it is safe to do so.

Follow Up

Try to follow up to ensure that appropriate action has been taken with authorities.
